1. What Is Lyophilized Royal Jelly Powder?

Lyophilized royal jelly powder — also referred to as freeze-dried royal jelly powder — is produced by subjecting fresh royal jelly to a two-stage process: first freezing it at low temperature to solidify the moisture content, then placing it in a vacuum chamber where the frozen water sublimates directly from solid to vapour, bypassing the liquid phase. The result is a dry, stable powder that retains the full bioactive profile of fresh royal jelly with a moisture content below 5%.

The key advantage of lyophilization over other drying methods is the preservation of heat-sensitive compounds. Fresh royal jelly contains a complex mix of proteins, fatty acids, vitamins, and enzymes — many of which are degraded by the heat used in conventional spray drying. The low-temperature vacuum process of lyophilization keeps these compounds intact, making freeze-dried powder the preferred raw material for premium health supplement formulations.

By weight, lyophilized royal jelly powder is approximately 3–4 times more concentrated than fresh royal jelly, reflecting the removal of water (which makes up roughly 60–70% of fresh royal jelly by weight). A 1kg bag of lyophilized powder is therefore the functional equivalent of 3–4kg of fresh royal jelly in terms of bioactive content.

Key Takeaway: Lyophilized royal jelly powder is fresh royal jelly with moisture removed by freeze-drying, resulting in a stable, concentrated powder that preserves the full bioactive profile — including 10-HDA, proteins, and enzymes. It is 3–4× more concentrated than fresh royal jelly by weight.

2. Lyophilized vs Spray-Dried: Why It Matters for Supplement Buyers

Two drying methods are used to produce lyophilized royal jelly powder for supplement applications: lyophilization (freeze-drying) and spray drying. For supplement manufacturers, understanding the difference is important because it directly affects product quality, labelling claims, and consumer expectations.

Property Lyophilized Powder Spray-Dried Powder
Drying method Freeze-drying under vacuum at low temperature Hot air atomization at high temperature
10-HDA retention High — preserved by low-temperature process Lower — heat can degrade bioactive fatty acids
Protein integrity Proteins remain largely intact Some protein denaturation due to heat
Colour Cream to light yellow Darker yellow to brown (heat effect)
Moisture content ≤5% ≤5% (similar)
Shelf life 24 months at room temperature 24 months (similar)
Price Higher (more complex process) Lower
Preferred for Premium supplements, high-potency formulations Cost-sensitive applications, fillers

For supplement manufacturers targeting health-conscious consumers or premium market positioning, lyophilized powder is the standard choice. The higher 10-HDA retention means label claims can be substantiated with COA data, and the preserved protein profile supports the full range of royal jelly's documented bioactive properties.

Key Takeaway: Lyophilized powder costs more than spray-dried but delivers higher 10-HDA retention and better preservation of heat-sensitive bioactive compounds. For premium supplement formulations, lyophilized is the correct choice. Always confirm the drying method with your supplier and verify it in the COA.

3. Understanding 10-HDA: 4.0%, 5.0% and 6.0% Grades Explained

10-hydroxy-2-decenoic acid (10-HDA) is a medium-chain fatty acid found exclusively in royal jelly. It is the compound most closely studied in royal jelly research, and its concentration in the powder is the primary quality indicator used by supplement manufacturers worldwide to assess and specify royal jelly raw material.

HANGZHOU AADON TRADING CO., LTD. supplies lyophilized royal jelly powder in three 10-HDA grades:

4.0%
Standard Grade

Entry-level commercial grade. Meets standard supplement industry specifications. Suitable for value-tier products and applications where cost efficiency is the primary consideration. Equivalent to approximately 1.0–1.2% 10-HDA in fresh royal jelly.

5.0%
Premium Grade

Mid-range grade preferred for branded supplement lines and health food retailers. Offers a meaningful step up in potency from standard grade with a moderate price premium. Well-suited to mid-tier premium positioning in the US, EU, Japanese, and Korean markets.

6.0%
High-Potency Grade

High-potency grade for maximum-strength supplement formulations. Preferred by brands competing on potency claims and by buyers targeting premium health markets where consumers actively compare 10-HDA percentages. Commands the highest price per kilogram.

How 10-HDA Grade Is Determined

The 10-HDA percentage in lyophilized powder reflects the quality and freshness of the fresh royal jelly used as the raw material, combined with the care taken during collection, storage, and processing. Higher-grade fresh royal jelly — collected more frequently, stored at lower temperatures, and processed more quickly — yields higher 10-HDA content in the finished powder. The lyophilization process itself does not add 10-HDA; it preserves what is present in the fresh material.

This is why 10-HDA grade is not just a specification — it is an indirect indicator of the entire upstream quality management chain, from hive to processing facility.

4. COA Standards: What Parameters to Require

A credible Certificate of Analysis for lyophilized royal jelly powder should cover the following parameters as a minimum. Buyers who accept COAs with incomplete parameter coverage are accepting an unverified risk in their supply chain.

Parameter Standard Specification Why It Matters
10-HDA content ≥4.0% / ≥5.0% / ≥6.0% (per grade) Primary bioactive marker and quality grade indicator; must match the grade specified in your purchase order
Moisture content ≤5% Controls microbial stability during storage and shipping; high moisture causes caking and accelerated degradation
Protein content ≥33% Confirms concentration level of lyophilized powder; low protein may indicate dilution with fillers or starch
Antibiotic residues Negative / within regulatory limits The most common cause of import rejection for royal jelly products in the US, EU, Japan, and South Korea; chloramphenicol, tetracyclines, and streptomycin are the most frequently tested
Heavy metals Within regulatory limits (lead, cadmium, mercury, arsenic) Required for supplement registration and import compliance in most markets
Total plate count Within specification Microbiological safety parameter; critical for supplement manufacturing environments
Yeast & mould Within specification Supplement GMP compliance requirement in most regulated markets
Colour & appearance Cream to light yellow, fine powder Darker colour or unusual appearance may indicate heat damage or spray drying misrepresented as lyophilized

A Note on Antibiotic Residues

Antibiotic residues — particularly chloramphenicol — are the single most common reason for royal jelly shipment rejection at customs in the United States, European Union, Japan, and South Korea. We require our partner factories to test every batch for antibiotic residues before shipment. COA results are batch-specific and available for review before you confirm your order.

Buyers importing into regulated markets should confirm with their regulatory advisor which specific antibiotic residues need to be tested for their market, as requirements vary between the EU, US FDA, Japanese MHLW, and South Korean MFDS.

Key Takeaway: A complete COA for lyophilized royal jelly powder must include 10-HDA content, moisture, protein, antibiotic residues, and heavy metals as a minimum. Antibiotic residues — especially chloramphenicol — are the leading cause of import rejection worldwide. Confirm these are tested batch-by-batch, not just periodically.

5. Supplement Manufacturing Applications

Lyophilized royal jelly powder is used across a range of health supplement formats. Its low moisture content, stable shelf life, and free-flowing powder form make it well-suited to most standard supplement manufacturing processes.

Capsules

The most common format. Lyophilized royal jelly powder fills directly into hard-shell gelatin or vegetarian capsules. Standard capsule sizes (00, 0, 1) accommodate 300–600mg of powder per capsule. For products marketed on potency, the 10-HDA grade chosen should align with the label claim — for example, a product claiming "6% 10-HDA royal jelly" must use ≥6.0% grade material and verify it against the batch COA.

Tablets

Lyophilized royal jelly powder can be compressed into tablets, typically with binders and excipients. The powder's light, slightly hygroscopic nature means that tableting facilities should maintain controlled humidity during manufacturing to prevent caking during compression.

Powder Blends and Sachets

Lyophilized powder is frequently blended with other bee-derived ingredients (propolis extract, bee pollen) or with complementary nutraceutical ingredients for sachet or stick-pack formats. The neutral, slightly sweet flavour profile of lyophilized powder blends well in honey-flavoured or unflavoured supplement mixes.

Soft Gels

While less common, lyophilized royal jelly powder can be incorporated into soft gel formulations, typically suspended in oil (such as olive oil or sunflower oil). This format is popular in the South Korean and Japanese supplement markets.

Key Takeaway: Lyophilized royal jelly powder is compatible with capsule, tablet, powder blend, and soft gel manufacturing. For capsule and tablet formats, ensure your manufacturing environment controls humidity, as the powder is slightly hygroscopic. The 10-HDA grade used must match the label claim on the finished product.
