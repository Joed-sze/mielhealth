1. What Is Royal Jelly and Why Import from China?

Royal jelly is a milky secretion produced by worker honeybees and fed to larvae and the queen bee throughout her life. It is one of the most nutrient-dense natural substances known, rich in proteins, lipids, vitamins, minerals, and unique bioactive compounds. For the health supplement, cosmetic, and pharmaceutical industries, royal jelly is a high-value ingredient with growing global demand.

China dominates the world's royal jelly production, accounting for the vast majority of global supply. Chinese beekeepers have decades of expertise in royal jelly harvesting, and the country's advanced processing facilities produce both fresh royal jelly and lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder to exacting international standards. For buyers seeking reliable supply, competitive pricing, and consistent quality, China is the logical sourcing destination.

2. Fresh Royal Jelly vs. Royal Jelly Powder: Which Should You Choose?

One of the first decisions every royal jelly buyer faces is whether to import fresh royal jelly or lyophilized (freeze-dried) powder. Each form has distinct advantages, and the right choice depends on your end-use application, storage capabilities, shipping budget, and order volume.

Fresh Royal Jelly: The Unprocessed Powerhouse

Fresh royal jelly contains approximately 60-70% moisture by nature, with a standard maximum of 67.0%, protein content of at least 11%, 10-16% sugars, 3-6% lipids, and a rich spectrum of vitamins (especially B-complex), minerals, and bioactive compounds. The key quality marker is 10-HDA (10-hydroxy-2-decenoic acid), a fatty acid unique to royal jelly that is linked to many of its health benefits. Premium Chinese fresh royal jelly consistently tests at 1.4% or higher for 10-HDA.

The main consideration with fresh royal jelly is temperature sensitivity. For optimal preservation of bioactive compounds, it is best stored at -18°C. While short-term exposure to higher temperatures during standard shipping is common practice and generally acceptable for well-packaged product, prolonged or repeated temperature fluctuations can degrade 10-HDA and other key nutrients over time. Fresh royal jelly is shipped by air freight, and we use the fastest available routing to deliver the goods to the buyer's designated airport. Sea freight reefer containers set to -18°C are available for orders of 1000 kg or more.

Royal Jelly Powder (Lyophilized): Convenience and Stability

Lyophilized royal jelly powder is produced by freeze-drying fresh royal jelly under vacuum, removing the water content while preserving the bioactive compounds. The resulting powder is concentrated, lightweight, and stable at room temperature. Premium powder contains 10-HDA ≥ 4.0%, protein ≥ 33%, and moisture ≤ 5.0%.

The most significant advantage of royal jelly powder is logistical flexibility. With an MOQ of just 30 kg and the ability to ship via DHL express, it is ideal for first-time buyers, small supplement manufacturers, and businesses in regions where cold chain logistics are unreliable or prohibitively expensive. The powder form also integrates easily into capsules, tablets, smoothies, and cosmetic formulations.

Key Takeaway: Choose fresh royal jelly if you have cold storage and need the unprocessed form for cosmetics or premium fresh supplements. Choose lyophilized powder if you need lower MOQ, room-temperature stability, and easier shipping — especially the unique 30 kg DHL express option.

3. Key Quality Markers Every Buyer Must Know

Not all royal jelly is created equal. Before placing an order, you must understand the key quality parameters that define premium-grade product and protect your business from substandard or adulterated shipments.

Certificate of Analysis (COA) Parameters

Every shipment of royal jelly should be accompanied by a comprehensive Certificate of Analysis from an accredited laboratory. Here are the critical parameters to verify:

Parameter Fresh Royal Jelly Royal Jelly Powder Why It Matters
10-HDA ≥ 1.4% ≥ 4.0% The primary bioactive marker; higher = better quality and potency
Moisture ≤ 67.0% ≤ 5.0% Excess moisture promotes microbial growth and degradation
Protein ≥ 11% ≥ 33% Major nutritional component; indicates harvesting and processing quality
Antibiotic Residues Negative Negative Chloramphenicol, nitrofurans, tetracyclines must be absent per EU/FDA rules
Heavy Metals Within EU/FDA limits Within EU/FDA limits Lead, cadmium, arsenic, mercury must be below regulatory thresholds
Total Aerobic Count ≤ 1000 CFU/g ≤ 1000 CFU/g Indicator of hygienic handling during harvesting and processing
Yeast & Mold ≤ 100 CFU/g ≤ 100 CFU/g High counts indicate poor storage or contamination

10-HDA: The Gold Standard and Pricing Factor

10-HDA (10-hydroxy-2-decenoic acid) is a unique fatty acid found only in royal jelly and is widely regarded as the single most important quality marker. It has been studied for its antibacterial, anti-inflammatory, and immunomodulatory properties.

10-HDA levels are determined by nature, not processing. The 10-HDA content depends on the geographic origin of the apiary, the nectar source plants available to the bees, and the harvest season — not on processing methods. This means buyers must clearly specify their required 10-HDA level upfront, as it is one of the fundamental factors in pricing. Higher 10-HDA commands a premium price because it reflects superior natural conditions during production, not a manufacturing upgrade.

Premium Chinese royal jelly consistently achieves 10-HDA levels of 1.4% or higher. Be wary of suppliers who cannot provide verified 10-HDA results or who quote unusually low prices for product claiming high 10-HDA content — this is a common red flag for adulteration or mislabeling.

Antibiotic Residue Testing

Antibiotic residues are one of the most common reasons for royal jelly shipments being rejected at customs, particularly in the European Union and the United States. The EU maintains a zero-tolerance policy for chloramphenicol in honey and bee products, and the FDA enforces similarly strict limits. Always verify that your supplier's COA includes multi-residue antibiotic screening by LC-MS/MS, covering chloramphenicol, nitrofurans, streptomycin, and tetracyclines.

Key Takeaway: Always demand a full COA covering 10-HDA, moisture, protein, antibiotic residues, heavy metals, and microbiological counts. Never accept a supplier's word without documented, verifiable lab results.

6. Common Mistakes When Importing Royal Jelly

Even experienced importers can make costly errors when sourcing royal jelly. Here are the most common mistakes and how to avoid them:

  • Assuming all royal jelly has the same 10-HDA content. 10-HDA varies significantly by geographic origin, nectar source plants, and harvest season — not by processing method. Buyers should clearly specify their required 10-HDA level upfront, as it directly affects pricing. Always verify the actual tested percentage rather than accepting generic claims.
  • Choosing sea freight for small fresh royal jelly orders. Unless you are ordering 1000 kg or more, sea freight reefer containers are not available for fresh royal jelly. Use air freight for orders under 1000 kg.
  • Skipping antibiotic residue testing. Antibiotic residues are a leading cause of customs rejection for bee products. Always include multi-residue screening in your testing protocol, even if the supplier provides a clean COA.
  • Ignoring storage infrastructure. While fresh royal jelly can tolerate short-term transit without strict freezing, long-term storage at -18°C is recommended to maintain optimal quality. Buyers who lack cold storage facilities should consider lyophilized powder for easier inventory management.
  • Paying 100% upfront to an unverified supplier. Standard terms are 30% deposit and 70% balance before shipment. Never pay in full before receiving and verifying your first order.
  • Neglecting to specify packaging details. Royal jelly powder can be packed in foil pouches, fiber drums, or bulk cartons. Ambiguity leads to receiving packaging unsuitable for your production line or retail requirements.

Key Takeaway: Avoid these six mistakes by verifying 10-HDA levels, choosing the right shipping method, testing for antibiotics, ensuring cold storage capacity, using secure payment terms, and specifying packaging in detail.
