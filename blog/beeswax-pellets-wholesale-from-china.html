1. What Are Beeswax Pellets and Why Buyers Prefer Them

Beeswax pellets — also called beeswax pastilles or beeswax beads — are small, disc-shaped pieces of refined beeswax, typically 5–10mm in diameter. They are produced by passing molten refined beeswax through a pelletizing process that creates uniform granules, which cool and solidify into the characteristic pellet form.

For industrial and manufacturing buyers, pellets offer significant practical advantages over beeswax blocks:

  • Faster melting: The high surface-area-to-volume ratio of pellets means they melt significantly faster than blocks, reducing production cycle times in cosmetic and candle manufacturing.
  • Easier measuring: Pellets can be weighed and dispensed accurately without cutting or breaking, reducing waste and improving formulation consistency.
  • Better storage: Pellets pour cleanly from bags or containers and do not require the physical handling that blocks demand.
  • Consistent quality: The pelletizing process requires fully refined beeswax as input, so pellets are inherently more uniform in composition than raw or minimally processed blocks.

For these reasons, beeswax pellets have become the preferred form for cosmetic manufacturers, candle makers, and food-grade beeswax applications worldwide — and represent the majority of beeswax traded in bulk export markets.

Key Takeaway: Beeswax pellets are the industrial standard for most manufacturing applications. If you are currently buying beeswax blocks and melting them manually, switching to pellets will reduce production time and improve formulation consistency.

2. White vs Yellow Beeswax Pellets: Which Should You Buy?

The most common question buyers ask when sourcing beeswax pellets is whether to choose white or yellow. The answer depends on your application, not on quality — both are 100% natural beeswax with the same core chemical properties.

Yellow Beeswax Pellets

Yellow beeswax pellets are produced from refined raw beeswax that has been filtered and purified but not bleached. They retain the characteristic golden-yellow colour and mild honey-like scent of natural beeswax. The colour intensity can vary slightly depending on the floral source and region of origin, but is consistent within a given batch.

Yellow beeswax pellets are the preferred choice for:

  • Natural and organic cosmetic formulations where colour is not a concern (lip balms, body butters, hair waxes)
  • Beeswax candles where natural colour is desirable
  • Wood polishes, leather conditioners, and industrial wax applications
  • Formulations where the natural honey scent adds value

White Beeswax Pellets

White beeswax pellets undergo an additional processing step — typically filtration combined with natural bleaching methods — to remove the yellow pigments and natural scent from the wax. The result is a bright white, odour-neutral pellet that is chemically equivalent to yellow beeswax but visually and aromatically neutral.

White beeswax pellets are the preferred choice for:

  • White or light-coloured cosmetic formulations where yellow tint would affect the final product appearance (white creams, light-coloured lip products)
  • Pharmaceutical applications requiring BP or USP grade beeswax
  • Food-grade applications where a neutral appearance is required
  • Formulations with delicate fragrances where the natural honey scent of yellow beeswax would interfere

3. Grades and Applications: Cosmetic, Candle, Food & Pharma

Beyond colour, buyers often ask about "grades" of beeswax pellets. In practice, the term "grade" refers less to a formal classification system and more to the intended application and the certification standards that apply to it.

Cosmetic Grade Beeswax Pellets

The most widely used grade, suitable for lip balms, lotions, creams, hair waxes, and body butters. Cosmetic grade beeswax must be free of heavy metals and microbial contamination, and should conform to recognised standards such as the European Pharmacopoeia or the specifications in the INCI (International Nomenclature of Cosmetic Ingredients) system. Both white and yellow beeswax pellets are commonly used in cosmetic formulations.

Candle Grade Beeswax Pellets

Used for beeswax candle manufacturing, including pillar candles, taper candles, rolled candles, and container candles. Candle grade beeswax is typically yellow and is assessed primarily on melting point, colour consistency, and freedom from adulteration. Buyers making premium or certified natural candles should request a COA confirming the product is 100% natural beeswax without paraffin or synthetic wax additions.

Food Grade Beeswax Pellets

Used as a food additive (E901 in the EU) for coating fruits, confectionery, and cheese, and in food-contact packaging. Food grade beeswax must comply with applicable food safety regulations in the destination market and should conform to food-grade purity standards. A COA covering heavy metals and microbial counts is standard documentation for food-grade applications.

Pharmaceutical Grade Beeswax Pellets

Used in tablet coatings, ointments, and controlled-release formulations. Pharmaceutical grade beeswax typically refers to white beeswax conforming to BP (British Pharmacopoeia) or USP (United States Pharmacopeia) specifications, which define parameters including melting point, acid value, ester value, saponification value, and absence of specific impurities. Buyers requiring pharmaceutical grade beeswax should specify BP or USP compliance and request the relevant test reports.

6. Sourcing Beeswax Pellets from China: What to Expect

China is one of the world's largest producers and exporters of beeswax, with significant production centred in provinces including Zhejiang, Shandong, and Yunnan. For international buyers, sourcing beeswax pellets from China offers competitive pricing, reliable supply volumes, and access to a full range of certifications — provided you work with the right supplier.

Lead Time

Standard lead time from order confirmation to shipment is typically 15–25 days, depending on production scheduling and the volume ordered. For orders requiring specific certification documentation or third-party testing, allow additional time for document preparation.

Shipping

Beeswax pellets are shipped as non-hazardous general cargo. For standard bulk orders (1,000kg and above), sea freight in 20ft or 40ft containers is the most cost-effective option. Beeswax pellets do not require refrigeration or special handling — standard dry container shipment is appropriate.

Documentation

Every shipment from HANGZHOU AADON TRADING CO., LTD. includes a Certificate of Analysis, health certificate, certificate of origin, commercial invoice, and packing list. Additional documentation such as MSDS (Material Safety Data Sheet) or specific import market certifications can be arranged on request.

Payment Terms

Standard payment terms are T/T (bank transfer). For first-time buyers, a deposit is typically required before production, with the balance payable before shipment. Established buyers may be offered more flexible terms over time.

What to Watch Out For

Beeswax adulteration is a well-documented issue in the global market. Common adulterants include paraffin wax, carnauba wax, and synthetic waxes, which are blended into beeswax to reduce cost. The key safeguards are: requiring a COA with acid value, ester value, and melting point for every batch; and sourcing from suppliers with verifiable HACCP certification and a track record of consistent supply.

Key Takeaway: China offers competitive pricing and reliable supply for beeswax pellets, but adulteration is a real risk in the market. Always require a COA with authenticity parameters (acid value, ester value, melting point) and source from certified suppliers with a verifiable track record.
