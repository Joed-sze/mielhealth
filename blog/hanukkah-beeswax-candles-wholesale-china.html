1. Why Buyers Choose Pure Beeswax Hanukkah Candles

Hanukkah candles –used in the hanukkiah (menorah) during the eight nights of the Jewish Festival of Lights –have traditionally been made from natural materials. While paraffin candles are widely available and less expensive, pure beeswax Hanukkah candles have maintained strong and growing demand among Jewish communities in the United States, Canada, Israel, Argentina, and Europe for several reasons.

Natural Origin

Beeswax is a natural product of the beehive, produced by honeybees. For many Jewish consumers, using a natural, non-petroleum-derived material for religious observance is an important consideration. Pure beeswax candles contain no synthetic additives, dyes, or paraffin blending –a distinction that retailers can communicate clearly on the label.

Clean, Long-Burning Flame

Beeswax burns at a higher temperature than paraffin, producing a brighter, cleaner flame with minimal soot and dripping. This matters practically for Hanukkah observance, where candles are left to burn for a required minimum time each night. The natural honey scent of beeswax also adds to the sensory experience of the holiday.

Premium Market Positioning

Pure beeswax Hanukkah candles retail at a premium compared to paraffin alternatives, reflecting their natural material and clean burn properties. For retailers targeting the natural products, judaica specialty, and gift market segments, beeswax candles offer better margins and stronger product differentiation than commodity paraffin sets.

Key Takeaway: Pure beeswax Hanukkah candles command a retail premium over paraffin alternatives. The natural origin, clean burn, and honey scent are the key selling points for retailers targeting Jewish communities and natural products buyers.

3. Hanukkah Candle Sizing: What You Need to Know

Hanukkah candles must fit the holders of a hanukkiah –the nine-branched menorah used during the Festival of Lights. Eight branches hold the candles for each night, and a ninth (the shamash, or helper candle) is used to light the others. Standard hanukkiah holders are designed to accept candles of a specific diameter, and buyers should confirm that their candles fit the most common holder dimensions sold in their target market.

Why 45 Candles per Box?

Hanukkah lasts eight nights. The number of candles lit increases each night: one on the first night, two on the second, and so on up to eight on the final night, plus one shamash candle per night. The total candle count for the full festival is therefore 44 candles (36 regular + 8 shamash). Our sets contain 45 candles –providing one extra candle as a practical allowance, which experienced buyers appreciate as it avoids the frustration of running short on the final night.

Size Compliance

Our Hanukkah beeswax candles measure 14cm in length. This size fits all standard hanukkiot sold in the major Jewish markets including the USA, Canada, Israel, and Argentina, and provides a slightly longer burn time than the minimum-size candles common in the mass market. Buyers should verify compatibility with any proprietary hanukkiah holders they supply alongside the candles.

Key Takeaway: A full Hanukkah set requires 44 candles. Our 45-candle box provides one practical spare. At 14cm, our candles fit all standard hanukkiah holders in the major Jewish markets.
